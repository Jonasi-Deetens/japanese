The alphabet

SeiOn 清音, meaning "pure sounds."

DakuOn 濁音, meaning "impure sounds."


Dakuten 濁点 are two little dots, or lines, also called ten-ten.

Handakuten 半濁点 is the open circle, also called maru.

This is just the Japanese way of saying that an unvoiced sound is becoming voiced. That's also why the "han" in handakuten means half. Just think of it as being half as pure.
